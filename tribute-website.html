About
Imran

Imran Khan is a Pakistani politician, former cricketer, and the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan (2018–2022). As a cricketer, he led Pakistan to its first Cricket World Cup victory in 1992. After retiring from sports, he entered politics and founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party, advocating for justice, anti-corruption, and social reforms. Known for his charismatic leadership, Khan remains a significant div in Pakistani politics, though his tenure as Prime Minister faced challenges, including economic struggles and political opposition.

Imran's
Journey

1952

Birth: Imran Khan was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan, to a well-educated and affluent family of Pashtun descent.

1971-1976

Cricket Career Begins: He made his debut for the Pakistan national cricket team in 1971.
University Education: Khan studied at Royal Grammar School in Worcester, UK, and later attended Keble College, Oxford, where he graduated in 1975 with a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics

1982

Captain of Pakistan Cricket Team: Imran Khan was appointed the captain of the Pakistan cricket team. Under his leadership, the team improved significantly.

1992

Cricket World Cup Victory: Khan led Pakistan to its first-ever Cricket World Cup win. The victory solidified his status as a national hero and one of the greatest cricketers of his time.

1994

Retirement from Cricket: Following his World Cup success, Imran Khan retired from cricket and shifted his focus to social causes and philanthropy, where he made the largest cancer hospital in pakistan, in lahore.

1996

Founding of Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI): Khan officially entered politics by founding the PTI party. His platform centered on justice, anti-corruption, and the empowerment of the poor.

2007-2008

Political Rise: During Pakistan's political turmoil, Khan's PTI began to gain momentum, particularly after his strong stance against military rule and his criticism of U.S. policies in the region.

2011

PTI Gains Popularity: PTI's massive rally in Lahore in October 2011 marked a turning point in Khan's political career, as he started to emerge as a major political force. His message of ending corruption and building a "New Pakistan" resonated with many.

2013

General Elections: PTI participated in the 2013 general elections and became the second-largest party by popular vote, though Khan failed to secure the prime ministership. However, PTI formed a provincial government in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK)..

2014

Azadi March: Khan led massive anti-government protests, called the "Azadi March," in Islamabad, demanding the resignation of then-Prime Minister Nawaz Sharif over allegations of election rigging.

2018

Prime Minister of Pakistan: PTI won the general elections, and Imran Khan became the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan. His government focused on economic reforms, foreign policy realignments, and welfare initiatives like the "Ehsaas Program."

2019

Foreign Policy Success: Khan garnered international attention with his efforts to mediate between the U.S. and the Taliban and his speech at the UN General Assembly, where he highlighted issues like Kashmir, Islamophobia, and climate change.

2020-2021

Challenges During COVID-19: Khan's government faced criticism for handling the economic impact of COVID-19, though he emphasized smart lockdowns and relief programs for the poor.

2022

Ouster from Office: In April 2022, Khan was ousted from office through a vote of no-confidence in the National Assembly, becoming the first Prime Minister in Pakistan's history to be removed in this way.

2023-Present

Political Struggles and Popularity: Despite his ouster, Imran Khan remains a powerful political div, with continued mass support. He faces various legal challenges and political opposition while advocating for early elections and reforms.

Achievements

1992 Cricket World Cup Victory

As the captain of the Pakistan cricket team, Imran Khan led Pakistan to its first-ever Cricket World Cup victory, cementing his legacy as a national hero in sports.

Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital

Founded Pakistan’s first world-class cancer hospital, providing free cancer treatment to over 75% of patients who cannot afford it, and making groundbreaking contributions to healthcare in Pakistan.

Namal University

Established Namal University in Mianwali, offering affordable, high-quality education in collaboration with the University of Bradford, UK, especially benefiting students from rural and underdeveloped regions.

Ehsaas Program

Launched the Ehsaas Program, the largest social welfare initiative in Pakistan's history, aimed at alleviating poverty through financial aid, scholarships, and employment opportunities, benefiting millions of underprivileged people.

Sehat Insaf Card

Introduced the Sehat Insaf Card, a health insurance scheme providing free medical treatment worth up to Rs. 1 million per family annually, ensuring healthcare access for millions of low-income families.

Kartarpur Corridor Inauguration

Opened the Kartarpur Corridor, which allowed Sikh pilgrims from India to visit one of their holiest sites in Pakistan without a visa, fostering goodwill and improving bilateral relations between the two countries.

COVID-19 Response and Ehsaas Emergency Cash Program

Led Pakistan’s efforts during the COVID-19 pandemic, implementing effective lockdowns and economic measures. The Ehsaas Emergency Cash Program disbursed financial aid to over 15 million households, mitigating the impact of the crisis on the poor.

Reforms in Education

Launched the Single National Curriculum (SNC) to bring uniformity in the education system, reducing disparity between public, private, and madrasa education and aiming to raise the standard of education across the country.

Housing and Construction Reforms

Initiated the Naya Pakistan Housing Program, aiming to provide 5 million affordable homes for low-income families, with subsidized loans and support for the construction industry, boosting economic growth and homeownership.
